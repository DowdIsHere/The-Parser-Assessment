Who This Is For

Practitioners working beneath presenting behavior.

CBI does not replace your diagnostic instruments; it works alongside them — identifying the cognitive input architecture beneath the patterns they already surface, so you can see why a client processes, responds, and relates the way they do.

Couples & Family Therapists Where the same misunderstanding recurs across sessions and communication-focused interventions have not resolved it.
Cognitive-Behavioral Therapists Where a client's architecture determines which cognitive frames reach them — and which don't, regardless of repetition.
Neuropsychologists & Rehabilitation Specialists As a clinical adjunct for identifying preserved gradient capacity and developing compensatory pathways in cognitive rehabilitation.
Executive & Performance Coaches For high-functioning clients whose patterns are not pathological but require structural explanation that behavioral frameworks do not provide.
Educational & Developmental Specialists For understanding how a student receives information before determining which intervention strategies are appropriate.
