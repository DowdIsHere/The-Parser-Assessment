The Premise

Not what you think. What you take in first.

A parser is the layer underneath preference — the filter your attention runs before you've formed a view. It's why the same meeting, the same dinner, the same argument leaves two people holding two different accounts of what happened.

I

It runs before you decide

Personality describes what you prefer. A parser describes what reached you in the first place — the raw intake, before judgment.

II

It doesn't drift

Preferences shift with mood, role, and year. The lens your attention defaults to holds steady. That's what makes it worth naming.

III

It explains the collisions

Most friction isn't disagreement about the facts. It's two people who never received the same facts to begin with.
