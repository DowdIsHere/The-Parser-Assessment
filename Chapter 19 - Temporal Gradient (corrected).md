# Chapter 19 — Corrected Temporal Gradient Section

*Drop-in replacement for the "Temporal gradient" definition + the "And the present?" paragraph. Resolves the contradiction: the present has no duration (true), but Present-orientation is a real direction of attention (which the 27 profiles require). Present = the balanced/seam orientation of the temporal axis — direction, not location.*

---

**The Temporal gradient — Past ↔ Future.** This axis runs along the anterior–posterior direction. The posterior end is oriented toward the past — toward what has already arrived, already been recorded, already happened: sensory registration of what just came in, and memory of what came before. The anterior end is oriented toward the future — toward what has not yet happened: anticipation, prediction, the forward lean.

And the present? Here we have to be exact, because the present is the most misunderstood point on any of the three axes. **The present is not a duration. It has no width.** As a span of time you could occupy, it does not exist — by the instant the "now" reaches your awareness, it is already a fraction of a second behind the event that caused it, already a memory. I am, as I write this, in the near future about to set these words down; the moment I do, they are past. You are forever *crossing* the present and never standing still upon it. Ask *"where is the present?"* and the honest answer is: nowhere. It lasts a millisecond and it is gone.

**But the gradient was never measuring where you sit in time. It measures which way your attention faces** — and that is the key that unlocks the present. A direction can point *at* the seam even though the seam has no width, exactly as "straight ahead" is a real direction to face without being a place you stand. So the three temporal orientations are not three durations to inhabit; they are three ways to be pointed:

- to face **backward**, toward the recorded — *Past-oriented;*
- to face **forward**, toward the not-yet — *Future-oriented;*
- to face **the arrival itself**, fused to each event as it breaks, reaching neither behind nor ahead — *Present-oriented.*

The Present-oriented person is not standing on a present that doesn't exist. They are *aimed at the incoming* — living in the body, in the moment as it lands, riding the seam rather than reaching off it. This is the athlete in the zone; the one who is utterly *here.* It is the balanced orientation of the temporal axis, exactly as *Balanced* sits between Concrete and Abstract on the Spatial axis, and between Self and World on the Reference axis. **Three orientations on every gradient: the two poles, and the seam between them.** The present has no location — but facing it is a real, distinct, lifelong way to be built.

And note what this is *not:* it is not "planning versus doing." Planning and doing are kinds of work. This axis is only direction in time — backward toward the recorded, forward toward the not-yet, or fixed on the crossing where the two meet.
