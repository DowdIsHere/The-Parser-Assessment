Internal · Marketing Playbook

How to reach every parser.

Same product, 27 different doors. Each parser takes in information through a different filter — the message that converts one will bounce off another.

This is not demographic targeting. It's cognitive targeting. Each parser has a preferred message format, an emotional trigger, a channel bias, and a trust mechanism. Match those — and the ad doesn't feel like an ad. It feels like someone finally understood them.

Actualized The Self-Made System

Respects competence. Ignores hype. Won't click unless the message earns it.

Message Format

Direct, no-fluff copy. Lead with the outcome, not the features. They want to know what it enables, not what it does. Short-form works — they'll dig deeper on their own if the hook is real.

Tone

Confident but not loud. Peer-to-peer, never salesy. Speak like a founder talking to a founder. Any hint of manipulation and they're gone permanently.

Best Channels

  • Podcast pre-rolls (founder-hosted shows)
  • LinkedIn thought leadership
  • Direct referrals from people they respect
  • Newsletter sponsorships (curated, not mass)

Trust Mechanism

Track record. Show who else uses it and what they built with it. Case studies > testimonials. Results > promises.

What Kills Conversion

  • Countdown timers and fake urgency
  • Over-designed landing pages with no substance
  • "Limited time offer" language
Example hook: "You already know how you work. Now there's a map for it."

Altruistic The Quiet Architect of Others

Buys for other people. The message has to show who it helps beyond them.

Message Format

Story-driven. Show a real person whose life improved. The Altruistic parser doesn't buy products — they invest in outcomes for people they care about. Lead with impact on others.

Tone

Warm, sincere, unhurried. No pressure. Let them come to it. They need to feel the intention behind the product is genuine — if it's profit-first, they'll sense it.

Best Channels

  • Community groups and forums
  • Word-of-mouth / personal recommendation
  • Nonprofit and mission-driven newsletters
  • Social media stories (not ads — organic shares)

Trust Mechanism

Mission alignment. They want to know why the company exists, not just what it sells. About pages, founder stories, and social proof from communities they trust.

What Kills Conversion

  • Aggressive retargeting
  • Self-centered benefit copy ("YOU deserve this")
  • Any sense that the product exploits vulnerability
Example hook: "Finally understand why your kid shuts down — and what to say instead."

Attuned The Frequency Reader

Reads the vibe of the ad before the words. Design and tone are the message.

Message Format

Aesthetically coherent. The visual design IS the first impression — if the palette, typography, or imagery feels off, they won't read the copy. Beautiful, calm, intentional creative.

Tone

Intimate and knowing. Speak as if you already understand them. "You've always noticed things other people miss" lands harder than any feature list.

Best Channels

  • Instagram (visual-first, story format)
  • Pinterest (mood boards, aesthetic alignment)
  • Curated email (beautiful templates, not mass blasts)
  • In-person events and experiences

Trust Mechanism

Felt resonance. If the brand feels like them — aesthetically, tonally, energetically — they'll trust it before reading a single review. Congruence is everything.

What Kills Conversion

  • Ugly or cluttered ad creative
  • Aggressive, loud copy
  • Pop-ups and interruptive formats
Example hook: "You always knew you were reading the room. Now there's a name for how you do it."

Centered The Ballast

Unimpressed by hype. Responds to calm authority and clear information.

Message Format

Clean, factual, structured. Bullet points over paragraphs. Present the information and let them decide. They respect a brand that doesn't try to manipulate their emotions.

Tone

Measured and professional. No exclamation marks. No urgency. Calm confidence — the ad equivalent of a steady handshake.

Best Channels

  • Search ads (they're already looking)
  • Professional publications
  • Long-form content marketing
  • Direct mail (physical, not email)

Trust Mechanism

Consistency. If the brand shows up the same way every time — same tone, same quality, same promise kept — the Centered parser will trust it. Volatility is a red flag.

What Kills Conversion

  • Emotional manipulation
  • Flash sales and artificial scarcity
  • Brands that change their messaging constantly
Example hook: "A cognitive profile based on how you actually process — not a personality quiz."

Coherent The Systems Integrator

Needs to understand the whole system before buying any part of it.

Message Format

Detailed and logical. Show the framework, the methodology, the structure. They want to see how the pieces connect. An "About the Science" page does more selling than the sales page.

Tone

Intellectual, precise, transparent. Explain the why behind the what. If you can show the architecture of the product, they're in. If it feels black-box, they're out.

Best Channels

  • Long-form blog posts / explainers
  • YouTube deep-dives
  • Technical documentation / white papers
  • Reddit (genuine discussion, not shilling)

Trust Mechanism

Transparency. Open methodology, visible framework, no hidden mechanics. They'll verify your claims — make sure they hold up.

What Kills Conversion

  • "Just trust us" messaging
  • Vague benefits without mechanism
  • Products that can't explain how they work
Example hook: "3 axes, 27 profiles, one coordinate system. Here's how the math works."

Collaborative The Force Multiplier

Converts through shared experience. If someone they respect uses it, they're interested.

Message Format

Social-proof heavy. Group testimonials, team use cases, "we did this together" framing. They buy into communities, not products. Show the people, not just the thing.

Tone

Inclusive, communal, inviting. "Join us" over "buy now." They want to be part of something — the product is the entry ticket to the group.

Best Channels

  • Group chats and shared links
  • Team-based referral programs
  • Social media (community-driven content)
  • Webinars and group demos

Trust Mechanism

Social validation. If 3 people they know use it, it's already sold. Referral programs and team discounts are engineered for this parser.

What Kills Conversion

  • Solo-user messaging with no social element
  • Products that feel isolating to use
  • No visible community or user base
Example hook: "The couples who take it together say the same thing: 'We finally have the right words.'"

Embodied The Hands-On Intelligence

Doesn't read about it — tries it. The best ad is a free sample.

Message Format

Short, kinetic, action-oriented. Video over text. Show someone doing the thing, not explaining the thing. If you can let them try it immediately (free question, quick demo), you've got them.

Tone

Direct, physical, grounded. No abstract language. "Take the first question right now" beats "Discover your cognitive blueprint." Concrete over conceptual.

Best Channels

  • TikTok / short-form video
  • Interactive ads (swipe, tap, quiz-style)
  • In-person demos and pop-ups
  • SMS / push notifications (action-first)

Trust Mechanism

Experience. Let them feel it. A free first question, a sample report, a 30-second interaction that proves the value. Words don't convince this parser — experience does.

What Kills Conversion

  • Long text-heavy landing pages
  • "Read more" without any interactive element
  • Abstract, philosophical framing
Example hook: "Answer one question. We'll tell you something about yourself nobody's told you before."

Empathetic The Emotional Translator

Feels the ad before they process it. Emotional authenticity is the only currency.

Message Format

Story-first. Real testimonials, real emotion, real vulnerability. They don't want polished — they want true. A single authentic quote outperforms a full campaign.

Tone

Vulnerable, warm, human. Speak from the heart, not the marketing playbook. If the ad makes them feel something, they'll remember the brand forever.

Best Channels

  • Long-form video testimonials
  • Podcast interviews (emotional, personal)
  • Instagram stories (real, unfiltered)
  • Email with a personal voice

Trust Mechanism

Emotional congruence. The feeling of the brand has to match the feeling of the product. If the ad feels corporate but the product is personal, they'll bounce.

What Kills Conversion

  • Stock photography
  • Corporate-speak
  • Any whiff of emotional exploitation
Example hook: "She said 'I finally understand why I carry everyone's feelings.' That's when I knew we built the right thing."

Equanimous The Even Keel

Immune to urgency. Converts on their own timeline, never yours.

Message Format

Informational, non-pressuring. Present the facts and let them marinate. This parser will bookmark it and come back in three weeks. Retargeting works if it's gentle — not aggressive.

Tone

Neutral, balanced, respectful. Don't try to create emotional peaks — they'll dismiss it as manipulation. Steady, consistent presence is the strategy.

Best Channels

  • Search (when they decide to look)
  • Informational YouTube content
  • Email drip sequences (slow, value-adding)
  • Review sites (they'll check)

Trust Mechanism

Long-term consistency. The brand that shows up the same way for a year earns their trust. One viral moment won't do it — longevity will.

What Kills Conversion

  • "Last chance!" or "Only 3 left!" copy
  • Overly emotional appeals
  • High-pressure sales tactics of any kind
Example hook: "A framework for understanding how you process. No rush. It'll be here when you're ready."

Foresighted The Horizon Scanner

Buys the future, not the present. Show them where this is going.

Message Format

Vision-forward. Roadmap, trajectory, long-term potential. They're buying version 3.0, not version 1.0. Show the ecosystem they'll be part of in two years.

Tone

Ambitious, forward-looking, bold. Talk about what this enables, not just what it is today. "This is the beginning of..." is a power phrase for this parser.

Best Channels

  • Thought leadership articles
  • Keynote / conference appearances
  • Futurist newsletters
  • Early-access / beta programs

Trust Mechanism

Vision credibility. If the founder can articulate a compelling future, they're in. They're investing in the vision as much as the product.

What Kills Conversion

  • Products that feel finished / static
  • "This is all there is" framing
  • No visible growth trajectory
Example hook: "Today it's a profile. Tomorrow it's the language every team, school, and family uses to understand each other."

Grounded The Bedrock

Wants to know exactly what they're getting, what it costs, and whether it works.

Message Format

Spec sheet energy. Price upfront, deliverables listed, timeline clear. No ambiguity. "Here's what you get for $20" converts this parser faster than any emotional pitch.

Tone

Straightforward, no-nonsense, honest. They respect brands that don't hide the price or over-promise. Say what it is. Say what it costs. Done.

Best Channels

  • Google Search ads (intent-based)
  • Comparison pages / "vs" content
  • Direct referral from someone who bought it
  • Pricing pages (they go there first)

Trust Mechanism

Proof of delivery. Screenshots of the actual report, sample outputs, money-back guarantee. They need to see the product before they buy the promise.

What Kills Conversion

  • Hidden pricing
  • "Book a call to learn more"
  • Vague, aspirational copy with no specifics
Example hook: "$20. Three questions. A 12-page profile of how your mind actually works. Delivered in 2 minutes."

Harmonious The Climate Controller

The ad has to feel good — visually, tonally, spatially. Dissonance is a dealbreaker.

Message Format

Aesthetically perfect. Clean layout, balanced composition, harmonious color palette. The design quality of the ad is a direct proxy for the quality of the product in their mind.

Tone

Gentle, balanced, inviting. Like walking into a well-designed space. No jarring elements, no visual noise, no tonal whiplash.

Best Channels

  • Instagram (curated grid aesthetic)
  • Pinterest (aspirational boards)
  • Beautiful email design
  • Physical spaces (events, retail, pop-ups)

Trust Mechanism

Design coherence. If the website, the ad, the email, and the product all feel like they come from the same universe, this parser trusts the brand. Inconsistency = untrustworthy.

What Kills Conversion

  • Visually cluttered ads
  • Inconsistent brand design across touchpoints
  • Loud, aggressive creative
Example hook: [No text needed — a beautifully composed image of the report with the cream-and-gold palette does the work.]

Idealized The Standard Bearer

Will only buy what they consider the best. Anything less than premium is invisible.

Message Format

Premium positioning. This isn't "a personality test" — it's "the definitive cognitive profiling framework." Exclusivity, craft, and authority. They buy best-in-class or nothing.

Tone

Authoritative, editorial, refined. Speak like the standard, not a contender. No comparative language — you don't compete; you define the category.

Best Channels

  • Premium editorial placements
  • Curated recommendation lists
  • Influencers with taste authority (not reach)
  • Architectural Digest-style brand storytelling

Trust Mechanism

Craft signals. Typography, paper quality (if physical), editorial voice, design details. They judge the standard of the product by the standard of its presentation.

What Kills Conversion

  • Discount codes and sales
  • Mass-market positioning
  • Anything that looks like it was made quickly
Example hook: "27 Parsers. One framework. The definitive map of human cognition."

Integrated The Whole-System Thinker

Needs to see how this connects to everything else in their life before buying.

Message Format

Ecosystem messaging. Show how the parser profile connects to relationships, career, parenting, team dynamics — the whole map. Individual features bore them; the integration excites them.

Tone

Systemic, interconnected, comprehensive. "This one framework explains your work, your marriage, and your parenting — because it's the same mind in all three."

Best Channels

  • Long-form content (blog, podcast, YouTube)
  • Cross-category marketing (appear in relationship, career, AND parenting contexts)
  • Ecosystem landing pages showing the full product suite

Trust Mechanism

Comprehensiveness. The more use cases the framework covers, the more real it feels to this parser. A narrow product feels incomplete — a platform feels trustworthy.

What Kills Conversion

  • Single-use-case positioning
  • Products that feel siloed or narrow
  • "Just for fun" framing
Example hook: "Your parser shapes how you listen to your partner, lead your team, and raise your kids. One profile. Every domain."

Intentional The Deliberate Mover

Won't impulse-buy anything. Give them the information and step back.

Message Format

Clear value proposition with full transparency. FAQ-style content works well — they'll have specific questions and want specific answers before they decide.

Tone

Respectful of their decision process. Never push. Present, clarify, and wait. "Here's everything you need to decide" is the perfect message.

Best Channels

  • Search (they'll find you when they're ready)
  • Detailed comparison content
  • FAQ and knowledge base pages
  • Email sequences that add value, not pressure

Trust Mechanism

Transparency and patience. The brand that doesn't rush them earns their permanent loyalty. Once they decide, they're in for life.

What Kills Conversion

  • Impulse-buy design patterns
  • Hiding information behind a paywall
  • Cart abandonment harassment emails
Example hook: "Three questions, one profile, full methodology published. Take your time — we're not going anywhere."

Introspective The Inner Cartographer

The ad that makes them pause and look inward wins. Reflection is the hook.

Message Format

Question-led. "Have you ever wondered why..." opens the door. They're already doing the self-examination — give them a framework for what they've been exploring alone.

Tone

Quiet, reflective, philosophical. Speak to the private conversation they're already having with themselves. No crowd noise — this is a one-to-one whisper.

Best Channels

  • Longform essays / Medium / Substack
  • Quiet corners of the internet (niche forums, book communities)
  • Podcast monologues (introspective hosts)
  • Email (personal, letter-style)

Trust Mechanism

Depth. If the brand demonstrates genuine understanding of inner life — not surface-level "know yourself" platitudes — this parser feels seen and trusts immediately.

What Kills Conversion

  • Shallow, buzzword-driven messaging
  • Loud, extroverted brand voice
  • Products that promise quick answers to deep questions
Example hook: "You've spent years mapping your own mind. We built a coordinate system for what you already know."

Intuitive The Pattern Jumper

Decides in the first three seconds. The gut says yes or the scroll continues.

Message Format

High-signal, low-noise. One powerful image, one sharp line. They'll either get it immediately or they won't — additional explanation doesn't help. Trust the first impression.

Tone

Confident, cryptic, resonant. A little mystery works — they enjoy the feeling of understanding something before it's explained. Let them feel smart for getting it.

Best Channels

  • Visual social media (Instagram, TikTok)
  • Short-form video with strong hook
  • Word-of-mouth from tastemakers
  • Ambient branding (they'll notice you in the background)

Trust Mechanism

Gut resonance. If it feels right, it is right. They don't need reviews or comparison shopping — they need the first impression to land.

What Kills Conversion

  • Over-explanation
  • Long feature lists
  • Anything that requires "reading the fine print"
Example hook: "27." [Just the number. Gold on cream. They'll know.]

Legacy The Institution Builder

Asks "Will this matter in 20 years?" before buying anything.

Message Format

Heritage-weighted. Founding story, the "why" behind the framework, the long-term vision. Position the product as something that endures — a family resource, not a one-time quiz.

Tone

Timeless, dignified, substantial. This is not a trend — it's a framework. Speak like something that was here before the customer arrived and will be here after.

Best Channels

  • Book-length content / published works
  • Institutional partnerships (schools, orgs)
  • Family-oriented media
  • Heirloom-quality physical products

Trust Mechanism

Longevity signals. How long has this existed? Who stands behind it? Is there institutional weight? The older and more established it feels, the more they trust it.

What Kills Conversion

  • Trendy, ephemeral branding
  • "New and disruptive" positioning
  • Products that feel disposable
Example hook: "The framework your grandchildren will use to understand themselves."

Mindful The Present-Tense Operator

Catches every detail in your ad — the good ones and the bad ones.

Message Format

Immaculately crafted. Every word, every pixel, every spacing choice matters. They'll notice the kerning. They'll notice the typo. They'll notice the stock photo. Get every detail right.

Tone

Present, grounded, precise. No future promises — what does this do right now? "Today, this is what you'll learn about yourself" beats "Imagine who you'll become."

Best Channels

  • High-quality print (magazines, journals)
  • Well-designed email (every element intentional)
  • Quiet social media (not stories — feed posts)
  • In-person, one-to-one

Trust Mechanism

Craft quality. The care visible in the ad tells them the care that went into the product. Sloppy marketing = sloppy product in their mind.

What Kills Conversion

  • Typos, broken links, misaligned elements
  • Low-quality images or video
  • Noisy, attention-competing layouts
Example hook: "Notice things other people miss? There's a reason. It's called your parser."

Reconciled The Bridge Between Opposites

Drawn to products that bridge divides. The "and" in a world of "or."

Message Format

Both/and framing. "Not a personality test, not clinical assessment — something else entirely." They love the third option. Position against false binaries.

Tone

Nuanced, inclusive, bridging. Acknowledge complexity. Don't oversimplify. They trust brands that hold contradictions gracefully.

Best Channels

  • Longform editorial (nuanced takes)
  • Cross-community platforms
  • Interfaith / intercultural spaces
  • Relationship and family media

Trust Mechanism

Nuance. If the brand can hold complexity without collapsing into simplistic messaging, this parser trusts it deeply. Oversimplification is the red flag.

What Kills Conversion

  • Binary positioning ("us vs. them")
  • Oversimplified categories
  • Brands that pick sides in polarized debates
Example hook: "You don't fit in one box. You never did. That's the point."

Reflective The After-Action Mind

Buys after thinking about it. Retargeting is your friend — but only if it adds value.

Message Format

Layered. First touch: plant the seed. Second touch: add a new angle. Third touch: the case study that connects it. They need time to process — give them new material each time, not the same ad.

Tone

Thoughtful, iterative, building. Each touchpoint should add to their understanding. They're constructing a mental model of the product over time.

Best Channels

  • Email sequences (escalating depth)
  • Blog series / serialized content
  • Retargeting with varied creative
  • Review and comparison content

Trust Mechanism

Accumulated understanding. By the time they buy, they've read everything. Make sure every layer holds up to scrutiny — they'll check.

What Kills Conversion

  • Same ad shown 50 times (diminishing returns)
  • Pressure to decide before they've processed
  • Shallow content that doesn't reward re-engagement
Example hook: "Remember that argument where you couldn't explain why you were right? Your parser can."

Reliable The Guarantee

Buys from brands that keep promises. One broken promise and they're gone.

Message Format

Promise-based with proof. "Here's what we'll deliver. Here's proof we've delivered it before." Money-back guarantees, delivery timelines, and clear expectations.

Tone

Dependable, consistent, trustworthy. The brand voice should sound the same everywhere — website, ad, email, support. Consistency IS the message.

Best Channels

  • Established platforms (not beta or experimental)
  • Search (when they're ready to buy)
  • Direct referrals
  • Professional associations and endorsements

Trust Mechanism

Guarantee. Money-back, satisfaction-guaranteed, "delivered in 2 minutes or it's free." They need to know the promise is backed by something real.

What Kills Conversion

  • Overpromising in ads, underdelivering in product
  • Inconsistent messaging across channels
  • Fine print that contradicts the headline
Example hook: "Your profile. Delivered in 2 minutes. Accurate or your money back."

Resilient The Rebuild Engine

Responds to comeback stories and underdog framing. Struggle is proof of value.

Message Format

Challenge-oriented. "Most people don't know how their mind works. The ones who figure it out have an unfair advantage." Frame the product as a tool for people who refuse to stay stuck.

Tone

Gritty, real, motivating. Not toxic positivity — earned confidence. They respect brands that have been through something and came out stronger.

Best Channels

  • Fitness / personal development communities
  • Motivational content platforms
  • Entrepreneurship media
  • Challenge-based campaigns ("Take the assessment, face the truth")

Trust Mechanism

Founder story. If the creator built this through adversity, the Resilient parser respects it. Origin stories of struggle > polish.

What Kills Conversion

  • Products that feel soft or coddling
  • "Easy" and "effortless" positioning
  • No sense of challenge or growth
Example hook: "You've rebuilt yourself before. Now build with a blueprint."

Seasoned The Earned Perspective

Doesn't trust marketing. Trusts experience. The product has to prove itself.

Message Format

Evidence-first. No hype. Show the results from people with mileage — not 22-year-old influencers. Testimonials from people who've "seen everything" and still found this valuable.

Tone

Respectful, mature, substance-over-style. Don't talk down. Don't try to teach. Present it as something worthy of their earned judgment.

Best Channels

  • Professional networks and peer groups
  • Book recommendations and reading lists
  • Advisory and board-level communications
  • One-to-one referral from a trusted peer

Trust Mechanism

Peer endorsement. The Seasoned parser doesn't trust ads — they trust people who've been around long enough to know the difference between substance and marketing.

What Kills Conversion

  • Youth-targeted branding
  • TikTok-style content
  • Anything that feels trendy or ephemeral
Example hook: "You've taken a hundred assessments. This is the one that gets it right."

Sentimental The Meaning Keeper

Buys things that mean something. The story behind the product is the product.

Message Format

Narrative-rich. Origin story, the moment of creation, the "why this exists" story. They want to know the human behind the brand. A founder letter converts better than any feature page.

Tone

Personal, meaningful, emotionally grounded. Speak like you're writing to one person, not a market segment. Sincerity over polish.

Best Channels

  • Personal email / letter-style
  • Founder-narrated video
  • Blog posts about the "why"
  • Gift-oriented marketing (they buy for others)

Trust Mechanism

Story authenticity. If the story feels real, the product feels real. They'll research the founder, read the about page, and check if the narrative holds up.

What Kills Conversion

  • Generic corporate branding
  • Products with no story
  • Mass-produced feeling
Example hook: "I built this because my son and I couldn't understand each other. Now we can."

Sharp The First-Read Closer

Three seconds. That's your window. Hit hard or lose them forever.

Message Format

Punchy, high-contrast, immediate. One line, one image, one CTA. No scrolling required. The value proposition has to land in the time it takes to read a road sign.

Tone

Direct, confident, zero filler. Talk like someone who doesn't waste time — because this parser doesn't have time to waste. Every word earns its place or gets cut.

Best Channels

  • Pre-roll ads (first 3 seconds)
  • Billboard / OOH
  • Push notifications
  • Twitter/X (short, punchy, shareable)

Trust Mechanism

Speed of delivery. If the product works as fast as the ad promises, you've earned permanent loyalty. Deliver the report in 2 minutes and they'll tell 10 people.

What Kills Conversion

  • Long-form anything
  • Multi-step funnels
  • Slow websites or laggy experiences
Example hook: "3 questions. 2 minutes. Done."

Visionary The Architect of What's Next

Buys potential, not product. Show them the world this creates.

Message Format

Big-picture, narrative-driven. Long-form is fine — if the vision is compelling enough to hold their attention. Show the ecosystem, the future state, the world where everyone knows their parser.

Tone

Visionary, bold, aspirational. "What if every team, every school, every family had a common language for how they think?" Paint the picture. They'll fill in the details themselves.

Best Channels

  • TED-style talks and keynotes
  • Long-form video / documentary-style
  • Founder interviews on big-picture podcasts
  • Manifesto-style landing pages

Trust Mechanism

Vision clarity. If the founder can see a future this parser also believes in, the product sells itself. They're buying the mission, not the feature set.

What Kills Conversion

  • Small thinking
  • "It's just a quiz" positioning
  • Incremental, cautious messaging
Example hook: "What if the next generation grew up knowing exactly how their mind works?"
