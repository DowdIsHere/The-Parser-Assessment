# Precision — What Each Metric Packs

*Every metric in the calculator is dense. It carries a **Block** (which CBI
processing block is load-bearing), a **Parser** signature (which orientations
express through it), and a **Physical** cost. This is why the **gap report**
matters: a shortfall on a metric isn't noise — it tells you exactly which part
of the architecture bent, and by how much. The gap report is the instrument for
recording **how much variance is allowed** per metric.*

Calculator keys: `ufc` · `r14` · `r9` · `steal` · `conv` · `oufe`

---

## Unforced Errors  (`ufc`)

**Block:** Perceptual Discrimination failure at execution — the HOW block
mis-calibrated shot selection or contact point. Sequential cost secondary — the
error collapses whatever point construction was underway.

**Parser:** Spatial-Concrete dominant players err when immediate geometry breaks
down under pressure. Temporal-Past players err when the situation is genuinely
novel and the pattern library has no match.

**Physical:** Low toll per instance. High toll in accumulation — unforced errors
signal that the body's execution system is degrading under duration or tension.
Muscle fatigue, grip pressure, footwork breakdown all show here before they show
anywhere else. The error is the body's first confession.

## 1–4 Point Rally  (`r14`)

**Block:** Spatial Relationships and Pattern Recognition are the load-bearing
blocks. The point is decided before Sequential Processing can build anything.

**Parser:** Spatial-Concrete dominant. Geometry read in real time, pre-loaded
patterns firing on contact. Temporal-Future parsers are slightly penalized —
projection requires time this rally doesn't give.

**Physical:** Explosive and terminal. Maximum output in minimum time — serve
velocity, return acceleration, first-strike force. Recovery demand is low per
point but the cumulative neuromuscular cost across a match is significant. These
points don't tire the aerobic system. They tax the fast-twitch system and the
nervous system.

## 9+ Point Rally  (`r9`)

**Block:** Sequential Processing becomes load-bearing alongside Spatial. The
player must hold a construction in time across multiple exchanges. Self-Reference
Processing enters — internal state management under extended stress is no longer
optional.

**Parser:** Temporal-Past drawing on pattern library. Temporal-Future projecting
two shots ahead. Reference-Other entering to read the opponent's defensive
posture across the length of the rally.

**Physical:** This is where conditioning becomes the deciding variable. Aerobic
capacity, lactic acid tolerance, hydration, leg drive in the fifth exchange — all
of it enters. The player whose physical system degrades first loses the cognitive
argument too, because Sequential Processing cannot hold a construction when the
body is filing a distress report.

## Point Steals  (`steal`)

**Block:** Pattern Recognition at the abstract pole — detecting structural
opportunity before it's offered. Generative Creation also enters at the moment of
execution — the shot required usually doesn't exist in the standard library.

**Parser:** Temporal-Future with Spatial-Abstract overlay. The player is
projecting into a window that hasn't opened yet and reading the shape of the
exchange, not the geometry of the current ball.

**Physical:** Highest execution demand on the list. The stolen point almost
always requires an extraordinary shot from a non-ideal position — full stretch,
off-balance, against the pattern of the rally. The physical toll is acute and
concentrated in a single moment. Shoulder, wrist, core rotation all absorb
maximum load simultaneously.

## Point Conversions  (`conv`)

**Block:** Sequential Processing paid off — the construction plan was executed to
completion. Social Signal Processing enters because conversion often requires
reading the opponent's defensive posture and committing to the finish at exactly
the right moment.

**Parser:** Temporal-Past recognizing the construction from the library and
closing it. Reference-Self holding internal regulation through the finish — not
leaking attention to the opponent's reaction or the crowd.

**Physical:** Moderate and controlled. Conversion rewards the player whose
physical system stays disciplined through the final shot — no over-hit, no
hesitation. The physical toll is about precision under fatigue, not peak output.
It's the body doing exactly what it was asked to do when it most wants to do
something else.

## Opponent Unforced Errors  (`oufe`)

**Block:** Pattern Recognition and Sequential Processing running simultaneously —
building a point architecture that puts the opponent's HOW block under impossible
demand. The inducing player isn't reacting. They're constructing.

**Parser:** Temporal-Future projecting where the opponent is going. Reference-
Other reading the opponent's architecture in real time and exploiting it. These
players are not playing the ball. They are playing the person holding the racket.

**Physical:** Lowest direct toll on the inducing player — they're controlling the
exchange, not surviving it. But the physical intelligence required is high:
precise placement, consistent depth, controlled pace variation. The toll is
cumulative and subtle. The player is spending physical capital on control rather
than power, and that discipline across a full match is its own form of
exhaustion.

---

## The complete picture
- **Short points (1–4)** — a physical and spatial event.
- **Long points (9+)** — a temporal and conditioning event.
- **Stolen points** — a cognitive and physical peak demand.
- **Conversions** — a disciplined execution event.
- **Induced errors (OUFE)** — a cognitive control event with the lowest physical
  cost and the highest parser sophistication.

---

## Why this sits next to the calculator
The gap report's two lines — *exceeds but short* and *beyond allowed deficit* —
are not just flags. Each one names a metric that is **packed** with the above.
When a side falls short on `oufe`, the report is telling you its
Temporal-Future / Reference-Other construction didn't reach the bar. When it
breaches the `ufc` cap, the body's execution system is confessing. Recording the
shortfall **is** recording how much architectural variance a winner is allowed to
carry on that specific block before the call breaks.
