## Running consistency watch
Things that must stay true across every chapter:
- **Three orientations per gradient** — two poles + a balanced middle (Spatial: Concrete/Balanced/Abstract · Temporal: Past/Present/Future · Reference: Self/Balanced/World). Present is the *seam orientation,* not a deletion. **⚠️ Ch.9 frames configuration as "weight toward A or B" and never names the balanced middle — must be reconciled or a reader infers 2³=8 types, not 3³=27.**
- **Parser ≠ personality** — a Parser is the *reception configuration beneath* the output; personality systems measure the composite output. (Stated cleanly in Ch.9 — make every other chapter honor it.)
- **Blocks are convergence hubs, not gradients** — a block is never "a position on an axis." (Ch.9: "Brain regions are not the gradients themselves… convergence sites.")
- **Gradients = reception (in); the conveyor = delivery (out).** The conveyor **funnels into the PNS** at the **sensorimotor/concrete pole of the principal (spatial) gradient** (Margulies sensorimotor↔transmodal). The hand is the proof: it *grabs* (motor — delivery out) **and** *feels* (sensory — reception in) — **one interface, two directions.** ✅ This *resolves* the earlier AP/rostral-caudal worry — the conveyor is the sensorimotor/PNS interface, **not** a second temporal axis. **Watch:** keep "conveyor (delivery)" from blurring with "concrete pole of spatial reception" — same real estate (sensorimotor surface), opposite directions.
- **Fixed architecture + learned incorporation** — positions don't move; bridges get built. (Ch.9: "Configuration Is Not Capacity.")
- **Anatomical claims:** confident for the lay/buyer audience; hedge to "maps onto known cortical gradients" if a passage is aimed at scientists/clinicians. **Weakest leg = reference/medial-lateral (no oscillatory citation; egocentric-allocentric isn't classically a medial-lateral gradient) — hedge it like the Margulies caveat.**
- **Mismatch, not deficit** (introduced Ch.1: "That distance is not a deficit. It is a mismatch.") — every chapter must keep difference framed as *architecture vs. method*, never as ability/rank. This is the same vow as Parser ≠ caliber: the gradients describe *what one sees*, not *how good*.
- **Binet lineage** (Ch.1: Alice/1887, the 1917 standardization that stripped self-reference, social perception, generative thought; 1909 single-dimension warning) — if Binet/the missing dimensions recur in later chapters, keep the dates and the three stripped dimensions stated identically.
- **Claude/origin disclosure** (Ch.1, footnote 1: Anthropic, March 2025) — the framework's stated origin. Wherever the origin is retold, keep it consistent and keep it in a footnote/aside, not load-bearing on the science.
